Agency Reporting Ops

Weekly Reporting Automation Checklist

If reporting eats founder hours every Friday, the team does not have a reporting problem. It has a systems problem.

1. Data Sources

Every client data source is mapped.Ads, CRM, call tracking, forms, analytics, and delivery metrics should be defined once.
Data pulls are automated on schedule.No one should log into six dashboards just to fill one report.

2. Metrics

Only decision-making metrics are included.Cut vanity noise. Keep pipeline, cost, conversion, delivery, and client-facing outcome metrics.
Definitions are standardized.If one account manager defines "qualified lead" differently, the report is already compromised.

3. Workflow

A recurring workflow assembles the report automatically.Fetch data, clean it, calculate the numbers, populate the template, and notify the owner.
Exceptions are separated from normal flow.Missing data should trigger an alert, not become a manual reporting day.

4. QA Review

One person reviews exceptions, not the entire report.Humans should check anomalies, not rebuild the whole thing every week.
Approval rules are explicit.Know what must be approved, what can auto-send, and what needs client context.

5. Delivery

Clients receive reporting through one consistent channel.Email, dashboard, Notion, Slack. Pick one. Stop improvising weekly.
Internal summaries are sent to the team automatically.Delivery and account teams should see the same signal without asking for it.

6. Time Saved

Measure founder/admin hours removed.The point is not prettier reports. The point is recovered capacity and fewer dropped details.
Reporting should be boring. If it still depends on one smart person remembering the ritual, it is fragile.
